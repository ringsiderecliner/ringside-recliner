> The match knew exactly how important it was — and became better by not pretending it was bigger.

This was supposed to be the match between the things people were waiting for. Instead, it became the thing worth waiting for.

Nothing looked designed for a highlight reel first. They fought over position, sold the effort of escaping, and treated a basic counter like information they could use later. By the closing stretch, the crowd understood the match because the wrestlers had taught it to them one choice at a time.

The best part was its size. It did not pretend to settle history. It gave two people a reason to care for twelve minutes, and that was enough.
